How Gene Drives Work

Turning inheritance on its head

  • Traditional inheritance gives a 50-50 chance that any given gene passes from parent to child. With a gene drive, that chance can approach 100 %.
  • This happens because a gene drive uses molecular tools to ensure that — when an organism carrying the drive mates — the “drive” version of the gene replaces the natural version, meaning both chromosomes carry the change. Future generations inherit the drive reliably.
  • In effect: one engineered organism can, over a few generations, spread a desired trait throughout an entire population — something traditional methods cannot achieve with such reliability.

Why It Matters for Medicine & Public Health

Many of the world’s deadliest illnesses — like malaria, dengue, Zika and others — spread via insects and pests. Traditional control methods often struggle with scale, cost, sustainability or ecological side-effects. Gene drives provide an alternative by targeting the biology of those vectors directly.
